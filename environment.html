Outdoor Photography

This collection focuses on capturing natural and urban environments through light, texture, and atmosphere. Many images are taken after rainfall, where softer lighting and reflections create a calm and immersive visual tone. The work emphasises observation of everyday details and how they can be framed to feel more intentional and expressive.

Featured Work

Wooden Butterfly

Concept: Exploring contrast between stillness and movement in a natural setting.

Technique: Depth of field and natural lighting used to isolate the subject while keeping background context.

Outcome: The blurred bridge and rain-soaked environment create a calm, reflective atmosphere.

Ladybug Study

Concept: Capturing small-scale detail within a larger environment.

Technique: Close-up framing and focus control to highlight texture and subject clarity.

Outcome: Builds on toy photography skills by applying similar precision to real-world subjects.

Process

Outdoor photography requires adapting to changing conditions such as weather and lighting. I often shoot after rainfall to take advantage of softer light and reflective surfaces. Composition is adjusted on location, with a focus on depth of field to guide attention while maintaining a sense of place. Unlike controlled environments, this work involves reacting to unpredictable elements in real time.
